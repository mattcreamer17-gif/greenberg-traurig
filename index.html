The question underneath

Pricing only pencils if the work has been compressed first.

Pricing sits on one side of Beekhuizen's desk and AI sits on the other, and the reason those belong together is simple. A fixed-fee engagement pencils only if the underlying work has been compressed first.

The firm's 2026 AI Outlook already says most of the hard part out loud. Accelerating regulation across the states and the EU, compliance complexity for businesses deploying AI, the supervision question under Model Rule 5.3. The question underneath is which pieces of a matter belong on the AI layer, which belong on traditional code, and which still belong on a second-year associate at nine in the evening.

That question has a name. The working model is 60/30/10. The audit that precedes any build identifies where each piece of a matter belongs, which is the part most vendors skip and call "strategy."

The frame

60/30/10. Where each piece of a matter actually belongs.

For a firm at GT's scale, misallocation compounds across 51 offices. Teams that skip this distinction spend AI budget putting an LLM where a properly indexed document management system would have been faster, cheaper, and more defensible under the ethics rules.

60%
Traditional code & data
Precedent retrieval. Template assembly. Clause libraries. Conflict checks. Matter intake. Structured data work that an LLM handles worse than a proper index.
30%
Rule-based logic
Deadline calculation. Conflicts resolution. Disclosure requirements. Regulator-matrix mapping. Deterministic logic where predictability and audit matter more than flexibility.
10%
Genuine AI layer
Open-ended analysis. Draft synthesis. Judgment calls under supervision. The slice of the work where an LLM actually earns its keep, with a human attorney owning the output.

The published methodology

The design is documented. Audit trail and reproducibility are the point.

For a law firm that has to answer to Model Rule 5.3 on supervision, interpretability and reproducibility are not decoration. They are the prerequisite. The methodology is open and cited.

Interpretable Context Methodology (ICM)

Submitted to ACM TiiS · MIT licensed

Agent context organized as a layered filesystem, from L0 identity through L4 working artifacts, with measurable interpretability and reproducibility. A 52-member practitioner community, a documented U-shaped edit pattern, and a repo anyone can audit.
